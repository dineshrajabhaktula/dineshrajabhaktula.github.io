About

Who I Am

I’m a final-year Electrical and Electronics Engineering student who enjoys building solutions that connect hardware and software. I’m motivated by understanding how electrical systems behave in real-world conditions and then using software to observe, analyze, and improve their performance.


My work involves renewable energy systems, power electronics, motor drives, control concepts, and embedded platforms, with Python playing a key role in transforming system data into meaningful insights. I enjoy breaking down complex systems, experimenting with different approaches, and refining solutions through iteration rather than relying only on theoretical answers.


Alongside my technical work, I actively participate in extracurricular activities that help me grow beyond academics. These experiences have strengthened my creativity, teamwork, discipline, and communication skills, contributing to a more balanced engineering mindset.


Through projects, internships, and self-driven learning, I’ve developed a strong focus on clarity, reliability, and real-world relevance. I’m naturally curious, detail-oriented, and learn best by building and improving systems. I’m currently seeking internship or entry-level opportunities where I can contribute meaningfully, learn from experienced engineers, and grow into a well-rounded professional in modern electrical engineering.
